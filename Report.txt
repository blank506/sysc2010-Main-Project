Signal Processing Methodology
3.1 Preprocessing

Raw sensor data may contain missing or corrupted values. These values are handled using interpolation techniques to ensure continuity in the signal. This step is essential for accurate filtering and analysis.


3.2 Digital Filtering

Digital filters are used to improve signal quality:

--Low-Pass Filter (LPF): Removes high-frequency noise.
--High-Pass Filter (HPF): Eliminates baseline drift.
--Band-Pass Filter (BPF): Isolates the frequency range of interest.

For example:

--ECG signals are filtered between 0.5–25 Hz to isolate cardiac activity.
--Respiration signals are filtered between 0.1–0.4 Hz to capture breathing patterns.


3.3Frequency-Domain Analysis (FFT)

--The Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) is used to convert signals from the time domain to the frequency domain. 
This allows identification of dominant frequency components, which are useful for detecting periodic patterns such as heart rate and breathing rate.

3.4 Feature Extraction
Features are extracted from processed signals to provide meaningful quantitative insights:

ECG: Heart rate (BPM), R-peak detection
Respiration: Breathing rate, RMS amplitude
Temperature: Mean value, variance, trend slope

These features help interpret physiological and environmental conditions from the sign
